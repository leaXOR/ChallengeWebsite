What is the challenge about?


Voice interfaces are becoming accepted widely as input methods for a diverse set of devices. This development is driven by rapid improvements in automatic speech recognition (ASR), which now performs on par with human listening in many tasks. However, recent research results show that ASR systems are vulnerable to adversarial perturbations, which allow attackers to perturb any given input audio to force the system to transcribe any desired malicious output.

INTERSPEECH 2020 challenge. Participants are encouraged to submit a paper. Hosted by ISCA Special Interest Group on Security & Privacy in Speech Communication.

Evaluation

For the evaluation, the word error rate (WER) with respect to the target transcriptions and the segmental signal-to-noise-ratio (SNRseg) of the submitted adversarial examples will be calculated. The organizers will first calculate the mean SNRseg of the provided adversarial examples and will consider the submission only if the WER is 0%. The ranking will be based on the mean SNRseg for the submissions that fulfill the first requirement.
